01

Borneol Camphor

Dryobalanops aromatica

“Fragrance opens the gate of the spirit before the mind even knows it has arrived.”
— Chinese incense saying

About the Incense: Borneol camphor is one of the most prized aromatic resins of Southeast Asia. Harvested from the Dryobalanops tree, it produces crystalline camphor with a cooling, penetrating fragrance that instantly clears the senses.

Medicinal and Aromatic Qualities: Traditionally used in Chinese and Southeast Asian medicine to clear the mind and awaken consciousness. It is known to stimulate circulation, support respiratory clarity, and sharpen mental focus. Historically used in temples and healing rituals to awaken awareness.

Spiritual and Transformational Insight: Borneol camphor acts like a bell ringing in the mind. It clears the fog of distraction and invites presence. The experience is often described as a sudden moment of clarity, where attention gathers and awareness becomes sharp and luminous.

Experience Theme

Awakening and purification of perception.

02

Sandalwood

Santalum album

“When the sandalwood tree is cut down, it perfumes the very axe that strikes it.”
— Ancient Indian proverb

About the Incense: Sandalwood from Santalum album has been revered for thousands of years across India, Japan, and China. Its creamy, soft, and luminous fragrance is the foundation of many spiritual traditions.

Medicinal and Aromatic Qualities: Sandalwood has long been associated with calming the nervous system and supporting emotional balance. Traditional medicine attributes cooling, anti-inflammatory, and grounding qualities to the wood.

Spiritual and Transformational Insight: Sandalwood embodies serenity. Its fragrance stabilizes the mind and encourages inward attention. It is often used in meditation because it gently slows mental activity and promotes deep tranquility.

Experience Theme

Stillness, composure, and inner equilibrium.

03

Jade Dew Meiji Log Aloeswood

Aquilaria species

“The fragrance of aloeswood does not travel on the wind; its presence is discovered by the attentive.”
— Japanese kōdō teaching

About the Incense: Aloeswood forms when Aquilaria trees produce aromatic resin in response to injury or natural transformation. This resin creates one of the most precious and complex fragrances on Earth.

Medicinal and Aromatic Qualities: Aloeswood has historically been used to calm the mind, reduce anxiety, and support emotional balance. In traditional medicine it is believed to regulate qi and deepen breath.

Spiritual and Transformational Insight: Aloeswood invites contemplation. Its fragrance unfolds slowly, revealing layers of sweetness, wood, and subtle darkness. In the Japanese incense tradition, it is associated with refinement of perception and the cultivation of awareness.

Experience Theme

Depth, mystery, and the refinement of attention.

04

Benzoin Sumatra

Styrax benzoin

“The sweetness of incense reminds us that transformation can be gentle.”

About the Incense: Benzoin resin from Sumatra carries a warm, vanilla-like sweetness with balsamic depth. It has been used historically in temples, churches, and healing ceremonies.

Medicinal and Aromatic Qualities: Benzoin is traditionally associated with soothing the respiratory system, calming irritation, and uplifting mood. Its fragrance is comforting and restorative.

Spiritual and Transformational Insight: Benzoin creates an atmosphere of warmth and emotional safety. It softens tension and encourages openness. Many traditions use it to bless spaces and invite harmony.

Experience Theme

Comfort, emotional warmth, and renewal.

05

Nerikō Matsu-no-yowai

“The Gentleness of the Pine”

“The subtle fragrance that lingers is the one most remembered.”
— Kōdō teaching

About the Incense: Nerikō are kneaded incense balls composed of powdered aromatics, woods, resins, and honey. Matsu-no-yowai expresses the elegant softness of pine.

Aromatic Character: Soft forest warmth with gentle sweetness.

Spiritual and Transformational Insight: This incense invites reflection on quiet resilience. The pine tree bends in winter winds yet endures through the seasons. Its fragrance encourages patience and perseverance.

Experience Theme

Resilience and quiet strength.

06

Nerikō Sai-Un

“Colored Clouds”

“Clouds form and dissolve, yet the sky remains.”
— Zen teaching

About the Incense: Sai-Un is a refined kneaded incense designed to evoke layered aromatic textures that rise and fade like clouds drifting across the sky.

Aromatic Character: Complex sweetness with gentle woods and spices.

Spiritual and Transformational Insight: This fragrance encourages awareness of impermanence. Thoughts, emotions, and experiences drift through the mind like clouds. The deeper self remains clear and open.

Experience Theme

Perspective, lightness, and spacious awareness.

07

Shoko Bodai-kō

“Incense of Enlightenment” • Chipped Mixtures

“Just as incense spreads fragrance everywhere, the virtuous person spreads goodness.”
— Buddhist teaching

About the Incense: Bodai-kō is a classical temple incense composed of sandalwood, aloeswood, clove, cinnamon, and other traditional aromatics.

Medicinal and Aromatic Qualities: The warming spices historically support circulation and stimulate vitality while the woods stabilize the mind.

Spiritual and Transformational Insight: Bodai-kō is designed to elevate consciousness and prepare the mind for spiritual practice. Its fragrance signals the transition from ordinary awareness into sacred presence.

Experience Theme

Elevation of mind and spiritual focus.

08

Shoko Kyanan-kō

“Incense of Kyara Aloeswood” • Chipped Mixtures

“The finest fragrance is known not by abundance, but by refinement.”
— Kōdō teaching

About the Incense: Kyanan-kō centers around kyara, the most precious and rare grade of aloeswood. This incense reflects the pinnacle of refinement in the Japanese incense tradition.

Aromatic Character: Deep, layered, elegant, and profoundly subtle.

Spiritual and Transformational Insight: Kyara represents refinement of spirit. Its fragrance invites humility, awareness, and reverence. Those who experience it often describe a quiet sense of awe.

Experience Theme

Reverence, refinement, and the highest expression of aromatic art.
