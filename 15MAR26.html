On March 15th, we invite you to the HōM Healing Center for a sensory exploration designed to awaken the senses and direct the mind. This experience is a curated path through the world's most sacred resins and woods, selected to settle the nervous system and invite a state of luminous awareness.

Presented by John Launius, Kōmoto, this journey uses fragrance as a bridge to focus. Each material is chosen not just for its aroma, but for its profound historical and spiritual ability to clear the fog of distraction and reveal the deeper self.

Borneol Camphor

Dryobalanops aromatica

Borneol camphor is one of the most prized aromatic resins of Southeast Asia. Harvested from the Dryobalanops tree, it produces crystalline camphor with a cooling, penetrating fragrance that instantly clears the senses.

Traditionally used in Chinese and Southeast Asian medicine to clear the mind and awaken consciousness. It acts like a bell ringing in the mind, clearing the fog of distraction and inviting sharp, luminous presence.

Theme: Awakening and purification of perception.

“Fragrance opens the gate of the spirit before the mind even knows it has arrived.” — Chinese incense saying

Sandalwood

Santalum album

Sandalwood has been revered for thousands of years across India, Japan, and China. Its creamy, soft, and luminous fragrance is the foundation of many spiritual traditions.

Embodying serenity, its fragrance stabilizes the mind and encourages inward attention. It gently slows mental activity and promotes deep tranquility, supporting emotional balance.

Theme: Stillness, composure, and inner equilibrium.

“When the sandalwood tree is cut down, it perfumes the very axe that strikes it.” — Ancient Indian proverb

Jade Dew Meiji Log Aloeswood

Aquilaria species

Aloeswood forms when Aquilaria trees produce aromatic resin in response to injury or natural transformation. This resin creates one of the most precious and complex fragrances on Earth.

Inviting contemplation, its fragrance unfolds slowly, revealing layers of sweetness, wood, and subtle darkness. It is associated with the refinement of perception and cultivation of awareness.

Theme: Depth, mystery, and the refinement of attention.

“The fragrance of aloeswood does not travel on the wind; its presence is discovered by the attentive.” — Japanese kōdō teaching

Benzoin Sumatra

Styrax benzoin

Benzoin resin from Sumatra carries a warm, vanilla-like sweetness with balsamic depth. It has been used historically in temples, churches, and healing ceremonies.

Creating an atmosphere of warmth and emotional safety, it softens tension and encourages openness. Many traditions use its comforting fragrance to bless spaces and invite harmony.

Theme: Comfort, emotional warmth, and renewal.

“The sweetness of incense reminds us that transformation can be gentle.”

Matsu-no-yowai

Nerikō • The Gentleness of the Pine

Nerikō are kneaded incense balls composed of powdered aromatics, woods, resins, and honey. Matsu-no-yowai expresses the elegant softness of pine, carrying a soft forest warmth with gentle sweetness.

This incense invites reflection on quiet resilience. The pine tree bends in winter winds yet endures through the seasons. Its fragrance encourages patience and perseverance.

Theme: Resilience and quiet strength.

“The subtle fragrance that lingers is the one most remembered.” — Kōdō teaching

Sai-Un

Nerikō • Colored Clouds

Sai-Un is a refined kneaded incense designed to evoke layered aromatic textures that rise and fade like clouds drifting across the sky. It possesses a complex sweetness with gentle woods and spices.

This fragrance encourages awareness of impermanence. Thoughts, emotions, and experiences drift through the mind like clouds, yet the deeper self remains clear and open.

Theme: Perspective, lightness, and spacious awareness.

“Clouds form and dissolve, yet the sky remains.” — Zen teaching

Bodai-kō

Shoko • Incense of Enlightenment

Bodai-kō is a classical temple incense composed of sandalwood, aloeswood, clove, cinnamon, and other traditional aromatics.

Designed to elevate consciousness and prepare the mind for spiritual practice, its fragrance signals the transition from ordinary awareness into sacred presence. The warming spices support vitality while the woods stabilize the mind.

Theme: Elevation of mind and spiritual focus.

“Just as incense spreads fragrance everywhere, the virtuous person spreads goodness.” — Buddhist teaching

Kyanan-kō

Shoko • Incense of Kyara Aloeswood

Kyanan-kō centers around kyara, the most precious and rare grade of aloeswood. This incense reflects the pinnacle of refinement in the Japanese incense tradition, offering a deep, layered, and profoundly subtle character.

Kyara represents the refinement of spirit. Its fragrance invites humility, awareness, and reverence. Those who experience it often describe a quiet sense of awe.

Theme: Reverence, refinement, and the highest expression of aromatic art.

“The finest fragrance is known not by abundance, but by refinement.” — Kōdō teaching
