Olfactory Performance Art

Inexcusable Evil

Translating the scent of destruction, trauma, and aftermath into a narrative incense experience.

The Concept

Originally created by David-Lev Jipa-Slivinschi for Toskovat, "Inexcusable Evil" is built as an anti-war statement. It captures the smell of a war zone—not to smell conventionally good, but to evoke a visceral reaction.

The discomfort is intentional. It layers violence (gunpowder, blood), medical response (iodine, bandages), and aftermath (concrete, rain, incense).

"You can get surprisingly close to the structure with incense—but the key is not copying notes literally. It’s translating the experience arc into materials that behave well under heat."

The Material Translation

A blend designed for temperature-controlled heaters, utilizing your expanded library of resins, woods, essential oils, and botanicals.

🧱

Structure (Base)

  • • Cedar bark (toasted slightly)
  • • Vetiver root (dry, not sweet)
  • • Sandalwood
  • • Oakmoss EO (lightly applied for damp, abandoned ruin)
🕯

Resin Core

  • • Black myrrh
  • • Honey/neglecta frankincense
  • • Labdanum (small amount)
  • • Copaiba EO (adds smooth, resolving balm)
🩸

Wound / Body

  • • Dragon's blood (symbolic sweetness)
  • • Saffron (tiny, metallic warm)
  • • Costus root (trace only, gives animalic "living body" effect)
  • • Patchouli EO (adds damp organic depth)
🏥

Medical Edge

  • • Galbanum (green, bitter)
  • • Borneol camphor (tiny pinch, sharp)
  • • Eucalyptus EO (clinical, sterile brightness)
🔥

Burn / Impact

  • • Nagarmotha (smoky earth)
  • • Cade oil (trace, charred edge)
  • • Guaiac wood EO (mineral, acrid, dry heat)
🌧

Rain / Air

  • • Dried bergamot
  • • Basil EO (very light, electric)
  • *Elevate with Mitti attar if available

Important Constraints

If you push the heat too high or heavy on certain materials, the blend becomes chaotic and suffocating rather than meaningful.

  • • Keep animalic materials (Costus root) VERY low.
  • • Keep burn notes (Cade) strictly controlled.
  • • Let the frankincense, copaiba, and air notes carry the heavy layers.

Realistic Expectations

You are not creating a literal replica, but rather translating the psychological trigger of the original perfume into the medium of incense.

  • You won't get literal blood or hospital.
  • You will get tension and unease.
  • A sharp contrast between sterile and organic.
  • A distinct sense of "something happened here".

A translation of Toskovat's Inexcusable Evil into therapeutic incense protocols.
