Juniper Tips

Juniperus communis – Albania

  • Used in Tibetan morning rituals to purify spaces and increase mental awareness.
  • The clean, green, and resinous scent evokes the pure air of a high-altitude forest.
  • Represents the element of Air and the spiritual breath of the tree, ideal for cleansing.

Rose Petal Powder

Rosa spp. – India

  • A cornerstone of Ayurveda, used to calm the nervous system and cool the body.
  • The aroma is believed to directly affect the heart chakra, fostering love and dissolving emotional barriers.
  • Its deep, heady floral scent creates an atmosphere of sacred intimacy and compassion.

“The rose is a rose from the time it is a seed to the time it dies.” – Rumi

Hyssop Powder

Hyssopus officinalis – Poland

  • Inheritor of the legacy of biblical "ezov," symbolizing profound spiritual purification.
  • A staple of European apothecaries, used as an expectorant and cough suppressant.
  • The sharp, camphoraceous scent clears the air and sharpens the mind for sacred work.

“Purge me with hyssop, and I shall be clean; wash me, and I shall be whiter than snow.” – Psalm 51:7

Jawee Resin

(Benzoin Composition) – Singapore

  • A traditional incense composed of three resins—benzoin, copal, and dammar—from Singapore's historic trade routes.
  • The name echoes the Arabic "lubān jāwī" or "frankincense of Java," a testament to ancient trade.
  • Releases a wonderfully sweet, honeyed, and balsamic aroma that is both grounding and uplifting.

“Within you, there is a stillness and a sanctuary to which you can retreat at any time.” – Hermann Hesse

Greek Monastery Incense – Acacia

Greece

  • Handcrafted on Mount Athos, following a 2000-year-old tradition of sacred perfumery.
  • Frankincense resin is elevated by the heavenly floral oil of Acacia farnesiana (Cassie).
  • Creates a serene, mild, and sweet fragrance conducive to prayer and contemplation.

Musk Seeds (Ambrette)

Abelmoschus moschatus – India

  • Known in Sanskrit as "latākastūrikā," the premier plant-based substitute for animal musk.
  • The aged seeds develop a rich, floral-musky aroma with wine-like or brandy-like notes.
  • Creates a warm, sensual, and intimate atmosphere that is subtly alluring and comforting.

Storax Bark

Liquidambar orientalis – Turkey

  • From a "living fossil" tree, a Tertiary relict whose lineage dates back millions of years.
  • Used by Cleopatra as a "love elixir" and by ancient Egyptians in the mummification process.
  • The scent is a complex floral-balsamic heart pierced by a sharp, medicinal top note.

“The wound is the place where the Light enters you.” – Rumi

Juniper Berries – Whole

Juniperus communis – Albania

  • Prized globally for their potent flavor, forming the essential botanical that defines gin.
  • A long history in European folk medicine as a powerful diuretic, antiseptic, and carminative.
  • The smoke is sharp, fruity, and pungent, representing the element of Earth and potent protection.
