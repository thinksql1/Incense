The Legacy of Baieido (Since 1657)

Among the most revered Japanese incense houses, Baieido has a history dating back to 1657. Unlike incense rolled on a bamboo stick, traditional Japanese incense is coreless, offering a purer scent with minimal smoke. Baieido is celebrated for its time-honored, handcrafted methods, blending fine natural ingredients like sandalwood, agarwood (oud), and traditional herbs.

A Brief Guide to Baieido Scents

The Kobunboku Family (The Signature Blend)

The most famous and accessible line, ideal for beginners. Based on a traditional blend of sandalwood, borneol, clove, and medicinal herbs.

  • Original Kobunboku: The classic sandalwood/herb blend for daily use.
  • Tokusen Kobunboku: An enhanced version with aloeswood for more complexity.
  • Kaden Kobunboku: A "family secret" recipe with Vietnamese aloeswood (woody, spicy).

Premium & Traditional Lines

For the experienced connoisseur, featuring rarer aromatic woods.

  • Shukohkoku: A high-quality blend of Indian sandalwood, Vietnamese agarwood, star anise, and patchouli.
  • Tokusen Shukohkoku: A more luxurious version with higher-grade, spicier aloeswood.

How to Appreciate Japanese Incense

  1. Place the coreless incense stick in a suitable holder.
  2. Light the tip and allow the flame to burn for a few seconds.
  3. Gently blow out the flame, leaving a glowing red tip.
  4. A delicate stream of fragrant smoke will be released.
  5. Always ensure the room is well-ventilated during and after use.
