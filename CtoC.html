Connecting
to

January 30  |  Drawn Studio

On January 30, the community gathered at Drawn Studio for an evening centered on creativity, presence, and connection. The space became a quiet, attentive environment where incense, live music, and art converged to support a shared meditative flow. The evening was guided by John Launius and Jacob Zengerling, with a thoughtful introduction and hosting by Ace.

John shared smokeless incense from around the world, each selected for its ability to settle the nervous system and draw attention inward. As the fragrances unfolded, Jacob created a beautiful, evolving guitar-based soundscape that served as a subtle yet powerful backdrop. Together, scent and sound guided participants into a quiet meditative flow state lasting approximately 1 to 1.5 hours, offering space to create, reflect, or rest in stillness.

Sandalwood

Santalum album • India

Sandalwood established the grounding foundation for the evening. Its soft, creamy presence supported emotional regulation and steady awareness, helping participants arrive fully and remain centered throughout the experience.

“In stillness, creativity remembers how to breathe.”
1
2

Frankincense

Boswellia sacra • Oman

Frankincense gently elevated attention without agitation. Its bright, resinous quality supported clarity and openness, allowing thoughts to move freely while maintaining calm focus.

“Clarity emerges when attention is unforced.”

Labdanum

Cistus ladanifer • Mediterranean

Deep and resinous, Labdanum encouraged emotional depth and release. Its warmth offered a sense of containment and safety, supporting a fuller engagement with both the creative and contemplative dimensions of the evening.

“What is held with care begins to soften.”
3
4

Aloeswood

Aquilaria species • Southeast Asia

Aloeswood introduced a quiet complexity. Its layered, evolving character rewarded patience and presence, mirroring the unfolding music and the gradual refinement of inner perception.

“Refinement reveals itself over time.”

Kyphi

Traditional Temple Blend • Ancient Egypt

Kyphi closed the evening by harmonizing the senses and integrating the experience. Traditionally used in evening ritual, it supported restoration and an easeful return from meditation into the shared space.

“Integration is where the experience becomes usable.”
5

Connecting to Creativity at Drawn Studio reaffirmed that incense, music, and intentional space are powerful tools for presence. The evening demonstrated how sensory practices can unlock creativity, support calm awareness, and strengthen community through shared attention and stillness.
