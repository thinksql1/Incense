What Is a Proxy?

A Proxy hosts a local group of attendees during The Incense Journey™, which is led live via Zoom by Komoto John Launius. Your role is to be a conduit for people to have a transformative experience with fragrance. You create a grounded, attentive environment, prepare the materials, and share the "way of fragrance" and the healing aspects of high-quality incense. This is more than just scenting a room; it's about guiding your guests alongside the Komoto’s presentation.

Remember to embrace challenges and not resist them. Be prepared to pivot if necessary while staying true to the purpose. Most importantly, have fun and approach the experience with great joy.

You are not performing or leading. You are holding space: with presence, care, and readiness.

Equipment & Technical Setup

Incense & Materials

  • Electric incense warmer: Golden Lotus is recommended (use setting 260 / ~325°F). For a Fireflower warmer, experimenting with the 270 setting is recommended. The goal is to warm the incense, not burn it.
  • Warmer Timer: Ensure the timer is set for a minimum of 2.5 hours to cover the 30-minute pre-heat and the 2-hour session.
  • 8 foil incense cups, pre-filled and ready in order.
  • Teaspoon, tweezers, and a spoon for materials.
  • Hand fan or feather (Japanese, bamboo, etc.).
  • Optional: Disposable gloves for handling sticky resins.

Audio & Visual Setup

  • Screen: A TV is generally better than a projector unless you can control the room lighting to prevent a washed-out image. Position it so attendees can see, but you can easily access the computer to type questions.
  • Audio: Prioritize sound. People are more forgiving of subpar visuals than poor audio. Bring a Bluetooth speaker or use a surround sound system if it's better than your computer's audio.
  • Optional: External microphone for better audio quality.

Technical & Zoom Readiness

  • Laptop/computer plugged in and fully charged.
  • Phone fully charged and nearby as a backup.
  • Zoom link, dial-in, and ID ready.
  • The Zoom call will be open one hour prior to the event. Technical support (Daniel) will be available to help troubleshoot. Daniel also monitors the chat for questions to relay to John.

Backup Plan

If your connection drops, immediately call into the Zoom session with your phone and notify the host.

Incense Preparation & Handling

Grinding & Quantity

Use enough fragrance to cover the entire bottom of your plate (approx. 1 level teaspoon). Grinding provides more surface area for a consistent fragrance release.

  • Freeze sticky resins for 24-48 hours before grinding.
  • Use separate coffee grinders for resins and non-resins to avoid contamination.
  • Some proxies prefer wearing disposable gloves when handling sticky resins after grinding.
  • Kyphi: Cut a small piece (pea-sized) with a knife; do not grind.
  • Wood chips & Borneol Camphor: Use as-is.

Plates & Warmer Use

Always off-gas new plates. Heat them on high for 15-20 minutes until any chemical smell is gone. Thin aluminum plates heat faster.

  • You can reuse plates for the same incense. To switch incenses, clean plates with high-proof alcohol (e.g., 190 proof Everclear) to dissolve resins.
  • Sources for plates: your warmer's supplier, "wayboat aluminum dishes" on Amazon, or Mermaid Magical Arts.

If the incense gets too hot and starts to burn, simply lift the plate off the warmer for a moment. You don't need to adjust the temperature. At the correct temperature, fragrance should release within 10 to 15 seconds.

The Journey & Attendee Engagement

Hosting the Journey

The journey is 2 hours long with 8 incense offerings (approx. 15 minutes each).

Pre-Journey Setup:

  1. Advise attendees not to wear perfume or eat spicy foods beforehand.
  2. Preheat your warmer for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Invite attendees to sit as close to the fragrance as they are comfortable.
  4. Join the Zoom call 1 hour early for tech checks. When attendees arrive (30 mins prior), let them settle in.

Sequence:

  1. Start the first incense cup as the session begins.
  2. Follow the Komoto’s cues for when to swap incense.
  3. Use your fan/feather to gently circulate the aroma; it's like a magic wand to direct the fragrance.
  4. Maintain a quiet, respectful, and attentive space.

Addressing Concerns

Support guests with gentle eye contact and open posture. Relay questions to the Komoto via Zoom chat. Do not prescribe experiences; let attendees have their own. If concerns arise, you can ask John to speak to them.

  • Anosmia (Can't Smell): Reassure them they will still benefit from the chemicals entering their body.
  • Coughing/Sneezing: Explain this is often a positive immune response. The incense acts as a bronchial dilator, opening airways. It's a productive cough, like an "internal body shower."
  • For Attendees with Chemical Sensitivities: Suggest they start at the back of the room and move closer if comfortable. High-quality incense rarely causes issues.
  • Disliking a Scent: Encourage them to "be with it" for a moment, as perception can shift. Rejection may indicate a block the incense is addressing.
  • Nose Blindness: Direct scent towards them with your fan, or suggest they gently shake their head to refresh their olfactory system.

Post-Journey

Be available for conversations after, as some may have questions they were hesitant to ask. Offer the used incense plates as gifts, with brief instructions on how to warm the remaining incense at home (e.g., on a stove burner).
