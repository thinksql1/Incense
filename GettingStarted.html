Getting Started: Your Essentials

1. Electric Warmers

An electric warmer allows you to gently heat incense without burning it, releasing its pure fragrance without smoke. This method offers control over temperature, revealing the subtle complexities of the aroma.

2. Foil Cups

These reusable foil cups are essential for easy cleanup. Simply place your resin or wood in the cup and set it on the warmer. Once you're finished, the cup cools and can be easily removed and reused.

A Note on Temperature

The numbers on your warmer's dial represent heat levels, not specific degrees in Fahrenheit or Celsius. Different materials release their fragrance at different temperatures. We suggest you play with the dial settings, generally between 200 and 270, to find the sweet spot for each material. For precise control and to find the perfect heat for each resin, you can use a grill surface thermometer.

Exploring Fragrances: Resins & Woods

Borneo Camphor

A powerful, purifying scent. Use as-is without any preparation. Add 6 or more crystals to a foil cup and warm at a lower heat (start around a setting of 200 on your warmer), increasing if you need more intensity. It will fully vaporize.

Explore Borneo Camphor

Benzoin

A warm, vanilla-like aroma. Works best at a lower temperature (a setting around 200-220) to avoid burning. Break off small, salt-sized grains or grind into a powder. For a cleaner grind, freeze the resin for 48 hours beforehand.

Discover Benzoin

Black Boswellia Sacra

A complex Frankincense from Oman. You can break off small pieces to warm directly, or for a finer powder, freeze the resin first before grinding to prevent stickiness.

Learn about Black Sacra

Honey Frankincense

A sweeter, softer Frankincense (Boswellia neglecta from Somalia). As the name suggests, it carries warm, honey-like notes that are deeply comforting. Break off small pieces to use.

Try Honey Frankincense

Sandalwood

The classic woody fragrance. Use chips as-is or grind into a powder for a stronger release. If you grind it, use the powder right away before its volatile oils begin to dry up.

View Sandalwood Chips

Red Sandalwood Powder

While less aromatic than traditional sandalwood, this powder has a subtle, earthy fragrance and is often used as a base in blends. Use as-is in your warmer.

See Red Sandalwood

Labdanum

A rich, leathery, and amber-like aroma. Freeze first, then break into small pieces for your dish. Melt evenly at a warm temperature. You can store it in the freezer and reuse it multiple times. A heavy hitter for trance and a crowd-pleaser.

Curated Incense Blends

Kyphi

A legendary ancient Egyptian blend. Break off a couple of small pieces, or a single pea-sized amount is plenty. It creates a rich, calming, and dream-like aroma.

Monastery Blends

Blends created for prayer and contemplation. To use, break off small pieces. For a finer consistency, you can freeze the blend first and then grind it into a powder.
