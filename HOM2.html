On February 15, we invite you to the HOM Healing Center for a sensory exploration designed to still the mind and open the inner sanctuary. This experience is a curated path through the world's most sacred resins and woods, selected to settle the nervous system and invite a state of steady awareness.

Guided by John Launius, this journey uses fragrance as a bridge to focus. Each material is chosen not just for its aroma, but for its historical and biological ability to clear the gate of the mind, revealing the quiet place that was always there.

Borneol Camphor

Drybalanops aromatic • Borneo

"Brain of the Dragon." Borneol does not invite the mind to wander. It clears the gate, stills the breath, and reveals the quiet place that was always there.

“Reveals the quiet place that was always there.”

Sandalwood

Santalum album • India

Its soft presence supports emotional regulation and steady awareness. In stillness, creativity remembers how to breathe.

“Creativity remembers how to breathe.”

Frankincense

Boswellia sacra • Oman

Elevates attention without agitation. Supports clarity and openness. Clarity emerges when attention is unforced.

“Clarity emerges when attention is unforced.”

Palo Santo

Bursara graveolens • Peru

Palo Santo does not impose stillness. It waits until what is false releases its grip, and only then does the sacred make itself known.

“Only then does the sacred make itself known.”

Labdanum

Cistus ladanifer • Mediterranean

Encourages emotional depth and release. Containment and safety, expanding creativity and contemplation.

“What is held with care begins to soften.”

A quiet complexity. Rewards patience and presence, inviting refinement of inner perception.

“Refinement reveals itself over time.”

Kyphi

Kyphi harmonizes the senses and restores. Integration is where the experience becomes usable.
