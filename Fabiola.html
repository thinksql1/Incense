My Heating Method & Supplies

Foil Cup Technique

I use bottlecap-sized aluminum dishes lined with food-grade aluminum foil for quick setup and cleanup with limited waste. I cut the aluminum foil sheets into little squares, just large enough to cover the top part of the dish completely. I save the spent resin in a mason jar and throw away/recycle the foil after use.

A Note on Aeration

Tip: Use a Fan

Moving the air makes a big difference in your experience, especially when first getting acquainted with the electric incense heater. Wafting the fragrant smoke can help you sense the different notes as the resin heats up. You can use any hand fan for this.

A. Ancestral Connection & Guide Work (Opening the channel, memory, devotion)

Opoponax

(Sweet Myrrh, Commiphora erythraea/holtziana)

  • Profile: Dark, honeyed-balsamic, “ancient library” vibe.
  • Why: Deepens lineage work, invites dreamlike imagery and counsel.
  • Heater: 320–375°F (160–190°C). Soften, don’t scorch.

Labdanum

(Cistus/Ladanum, Cistus ladaniferus)

  • Profile: Leathery, ambered, solar; thick presence.
  • Why: Stabilizes trance while keeping heart open; “remembers” through the body.
  • Heater: 285–355°F (140–180°C). Note: very sticky.

Mastic

(Chios mastic, Pistacia lentiscus)

  • Profile: Bright, lemon-pine, silvery clarity.
  • Why: Clears mental fog without “banish” aggression; great for receiving messages.
  • Heater: 160–230°F (70–110°C). Low and gentle preserves its crystalline sparkle.

B. Entity/Energy Clearing & Boundary-Setting (Cutting, bright, protective)

Galbanum

(Ferula galbaniflua)

  • Profile: Green, forest-resin, piercing clarity; notoriously sticky.
  • Why: Slices through murk; excellent for “edge” + protection.
  • Heater: 160–250°F (70–120°C). Start low. Note: very sticky.
View Product

Elemi

(Canarium luzonicum)

  • Profile: Citrus-lemon, pepper-balsam; bright and vertical.
  • Why: Lifts and clears without heaviness; great post-banish reset.
  • Heater: 195–285°F (90–140°C).
View Product

Dragon’s Blood

(Daemonorops draco palm resin, not dye-treated)

  • Profile: Dry, resinous-spicy, “red seal.”
  • Why: Classic for firm boundaries/banishing when things feel intrusive.
  • Heater: 300–360°F (150–185°C). Scorch-prone, use small pinches.

  • Why: Clears “sticky karma”/residues; pairs well after galbanum.
  • Heater: 300–360°F (150–185°C). Note: very tarry.

D. Meditation & Sleep Support (Calming the mind and body)

For Calming the Monkey Mind (Meditation)

Copal Negro

(Agathis dammara – Black Copal, Philippines)

  • Effect: Grounding, heavy, mysterious, and balsamic.
  • Purpose: Anchors the wandering mind and connects to the “deeper levels of inner spirit.”
  • How it helps: Its dense, resinous aroma provides a deep sensory anchor, ideal when the mind is restless.
  • Heater: ~250–300°F (120–150°C).
View Product

Eritrean Frankincense

(Boswellia papyrifera)

  • Effect: Sweet, fresh lemon-pine notes that slow and deepen breathing.
  • Purpose: Clarifies thought, calms nerves, and enhances mental perception and consciousness.
  • How it helps: The aroma’s physiological impact encourages slow, deep breaths, quieting mental overactivity.
  • Heater: ~220–275°F (105–135°C).
View Product

Maydi Superior Frankincense

(Boswellia frereana)

  • Effect: Light and lively top notes that evolve into a deep, balsamic base.
  • Purpose: Supports dynamic meditation. Begins bright to clear thoughts, deepens into grounding stability.
  • Heater: 220–300°F (105–150°C).
View Product

Zukoh Rubbing Incense

(Baieido)

  • Effect: Spicy and woody (Sandalwood, Cinnamon, Clove).
  • Purpose: Ritual purification before meditation; creates a sensory trigger for stillness.
  • How to use: Rub into hands before meditating (not heated).

Recommended Pairing for Meditation

Start with Zukoh Rubbing Incense as a ritual, then warm Eritrean Frankincense or Copal Negro during meditation to stabilize focus and silence internal chatter.

For Sleep Maintenance (Staying Asleep)

Guggul Resin

(Commiphora mukul)

  • Effect: Warm, slightly sweet, herbaceous with hints of vanilla.
  • Purpose: Evening relaxation and stress release.
  • How it helps: Traditionally used in India “in the evening to relax before retiring.”
  • Heater: 220–275°F (105–135°C).
View Product

Spikenard Root Powder

(Nardostachys jatamansi)

  • Effect: Deep, earthy, musky (similar to valerian).
  • Purpose: Calms nerves, strengthens the mind, stabilizes the nervous system.
  • How it helps: Potent nervine. Addresses the root cause of night waking by reducing nervous tension.
  • Heater: 180–220°F (80–105°C).
View Product

Mugwort Powder

(Artemisia vulgaris)

  • Effect: Strong, sweet, earthy, and calming.
  • Purpose: Promotes restful sleep and vivid dreams.
  • How it helps: Relieves tension and anxiety; promotes relaxation before bed.
  • Heater: 180–220°F (80–105°C).
View Product

Ancient Egyptian Kyphi

(SoE Blend)

  • Effect: Sweet notes of wine, honey, raisins, and woods that evolve slowly.
  • Purpose: Evening ritual incense to unwind and release the stresses of the day.
  • How it helps: A long aromatic journey ideal for preparing the mind and body for sleep.
  • Heater: 200–250°F (95–120°C).
View Product

Recommended Pairing for Sleep Maintenance

Before bed, gently vaporize Spikenard or Guggul to relax the nervous system. For a more ritualistic unwind, use Kyphi—especially if you want a gentle, soothing aroma that lingers through the night.
